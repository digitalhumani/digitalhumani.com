How it works

We provide simple and easy to use Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) to help connect websites and mobile applications to trusted reforestation organizations to have trees planted.

1- Choose a reforestation project

Choose a reforestation project from our list, in various countries.

2- Add code to your website or app

Add a few line of code in your website or mobile app to call the API requesting the planting of a tree.

3- A user makes an action

When your users make the action of your choice (buying an item in a mobile game, subscribing to online billing, buying a specific product on your website, etc.), a request to plant a tree will be made.

4- A tree is planted!

That's it! You will be billed ($1 per tree) by the trusted reforestation organizations of your choice. You will receive a monthly invoice and report indicating how many trees were planted on your behalf.


The solution is completely free of charge.

It is developed and maintained by a group of volunteers who want to make their part to help fight climate change.

The $1 / tree is paid directly to the reforestation organization you choose, without us handling the money. This way, you can be sure that the money will be used to have the trees planted.

Dashboard

The DigitalHumani dashboard is the quickest way to get started planting trees. From the dashboard, you can submit tree planting requests, view stats around your tree planting usage, retrieve info about the API including access info and documentation, and more.

Trees fight climate change by absorbing CO2 from the atmosphere.

Each tree can absorb 22kg (50 pounds) of CO2 per year for 30 years.

Furthermore, trees contribute to economic growth, poverty alleviation, food security and biodiversity conservation.

Use cases for your business

Here are some examples of use cases where you might want to have a tree planted:


  • When a user makes an in-app purchase in your mobile game

  • When a user switches to online billing

  • When a user subscribes to a 6 months plan

  • When a user buys a specific product on your website

  • When a user refers a friend

  • etc.


These are only examples, of course. It's up to you to decide when a request for a tree will be made, depending of which action, product or service you want to promote.

Reforestation Partners

The RaaS (Reforestation as a Service) solution currently works with 7 reforestation organizations

One Tree Planted is a non-profit organization with over 2.5 millions trees planted since their foundation in 2014. They are based in Vermont, USA. They currently have reforestation projects in the following countries, from which you can have your trees planted: Australia, Brazil, Canada (British Columbia, New Brunswick, Ontario, Québec), Colombia, Côte d'Ivoire, Denmark, Ethiopia, Ghana, Guatemala, Haiti, Honduras, Iceland, India, Indonesia, Kenya, Malawi, Mexico, New Zealand, Peru, Philippines, Romania, Rwanda, Spain, Tanzania, Uganda, United States (Appalachia, California, Oregon, Florida) and Vietnam. Details on the projects here.

Note: Because of an unusual high demands for trees in the last few months, availability of certain projects will have to be confirmed.

One Tree Planted is a Registered 501(c)(3) charity in the US. Their latest annual report (2019) available here.




Chase Africa works with a local partner and the Kenya Forest Service to restore the degraded forests on the slopes of Mount Kenya. The Mount Kenya forest supports a vast array of flora and fauna and is an UNESCO World Heritage Site.

DigitalHumani partners with Chase Africa for their reforestation efforts on Mount Kenya forest, and through the RaaS solution, you can have trees planted by Chase Africa in this project in Kenya. So far, 66,000 indigenous trees were restored on Mount Kenya forest by Chase Africa. Details on the project in Kenya (section Forest Restoration) here.

Chase Africa is a Registered Charity in the UK (No: 1082958) and their annual reports are available here.

TIST (The International Small Group and Tree Planting Program) encourages small groups of subsistence farmers to improve their local environment and farms by planting and maintaining trees on degraded and/or unused land. Over 88,000 farmers in 4 countries have successfully planted more than 18 millions trees since their foundation in 1999. Through the RaaS solution, you can have trees planted by the TIST farmers in Kenya and India. Details on the project in India here and on the project in Kenya here.

TIST is a project, run through a public-private partnership. Tax-deductible donations to I4EI, TIST's non-profit arm and a registered 501(c)(3) charity in the US, go directly to planting trees. Details here.


Sustainable Harvest International was built on the idea that environmental devastation and rural poverty are unavoidably linked. They partner with farmers, providing long-term support and hands-on training. Since 1997, they've worked with over 2,891 families and planted 4 million trees. Through the RaaS solution, you can have trees planted by the Sustainable Harvest International farmers in Honduras and Belize. Details on these 2 projects here.

Sustainable Harvest International is a Registered 501(c)(3) charity in the US, based in Boston, Massachusetts. 501(c) certificate and annual report available here.


Conserve Natural Forests is a non-profit foundation focused on forest restoration and wildlife conservation in the tropical mountain forests of northern Thailand. This includes the restoration of nearby degraded landscapes and the preservation of local forests.

More information about their reforestation work here.


Planting on Demand is a company offering mangrove reforestation via the Marine Conservation Philippines (non-profit in the Philippines) and via CarbonEthics (non-profit in Indonesia). Planting on Demand can send pictures that show the planting area before, while and after planting, including pictures of a tree planter holding a sign with the name of your company on it.

More information about their reforestation work here.


Community Forests International is a Canadian charity, working to protect and restore the climate by enabling communities and forests to thrive together. DigitalHumani's RaaS (Reforestation as a Service) solution can be used to plant trees in Acadian forests in Canada (primarily New Brunswick and Nova Scotia) on lands owned by Community Forests International. These lands are purchased for the purpose of bringing healthy forests back permanently and for the next generation.

Details on the Acadian project available here and their annual reports are available here.
